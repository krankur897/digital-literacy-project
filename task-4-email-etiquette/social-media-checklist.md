**Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist**

-   **Introduction**

> Nowadays, social media is a big part of students' everyday life. We
> use it for chatting, sharing ideas, learning new things, and also for
> entertainment. But at the same time, if we are not careful, it can
> create problems like privacy issues, cyberbullying, or even affect our
> reputation. So, it becomes important for students to use social media
> in a careful and responsible way. Below are some simple do's and
> don'ts that every college student should keep in mind.

-   **Do's for College Student**

1\. [Be Careful About Privacy]{.underline}

Students should check their privacy settings from time to time and make
sure their personal information is only visible to people they trust.

2\. [Check Information Before Sharing]{.underline}

Before posting or forwarding anything, it is better to confirm whether
it is true or not. Sharing wrong information can create confusion.

3\. [Use Social Media for Growth]{.underline}

Social media can be useful if used properly. Students can share their
skills, projects, and achievements, which may help them in the future.

4\. [Think Before You Post]{.underline}

Once something is posted online, it can stay there for a long time. So
it is always better to think before posting anything.

5\. [Be Respectful Online]{.underline}

While talking to others online, students should use polite language and
behave respectfully. It shows a good attitude.

-   **Don'ts for College Student**

1[. Do Not Get Involved in Cyberbullying]{.underline}

> Students should never post hurtful comments, make fun of others, or
> spread hate online.
>
> 2\. [Avoid Sharing Personal Details]{.underline}
>
> Information like phone number, address, passwords, or bank details
> should not be shared on social media.
>
> 3\. [Do Not Copy Others' Work]{.underline}
>
> Students should not copy content from others without giving credit. It
> is always better to be honest in your work.
>
> 4\. [Avoid Posting Negative Content]{.underline}
>
> Posting negative or offensive things about college, teachers, or
> friends can create a bad image.
>
> 5\. [Do Not Click on Unknown Links]{.underline}
>
> Students should be careful and avoid clicking or sharing unknown
> links, as they may not be safe.

-   **Conclusion**

Social media is helpful if we use it in the right way. By following
these simple do's and don'ts, students can stay safe, build a positive
image, and avoid unnecessary risks. It is important to use social media
carefully and responsibly, as it plays a big role in shaping our
personal and professional life.
